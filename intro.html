Natures Survival Experts


This project explores how animals adapt to environments that have been altered by human activity and extreme environmental changes such as pollution, climate change, wildfires, and urbanization. The investigation focuses on species that developed behavioral, physical, or genetic adaptations in order to survive in these modified ecosystems. Examples include the peppered moth adapting to industrial pollution, the black fire beetle responding to forest fires, the Atlantic killifish surviving in contaminated waters, the snowshoe hare adjusting to changing temperatures, and the wood frog surviving extreme cold conditions. The project applies biological concepts such as adaptation, natural selection, evolution, camouflage, and ecosystem interaction. It may also include mathematical comparisons of temperature changes, pollution levels, or population variation to better understand the effects of environmental change on animal survival.
